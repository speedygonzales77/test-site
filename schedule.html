Surfing Events

Below are the rules and regulations, please read them before scheduling

  • Respect the Waves: Encourage surfers to respect the local surf spot, its environment, and fellow surfers, avoiding aggressive behavior or disrupting others' waves.
  • Age and Skill Categories: Divide participants into appropriate categories based on age and skill level to ensure fair competition.
  • Age
    • Junior Surfers (Must have parent/guardian): Typically aged 15 and under, this category is for young surfers who are relatively new to the sport and still developing their skills.
    • Teens / Youth Surfers: Usually between 16 and 19 years old, this category may include both intermediate and advanced surfers who have been honing their skills for a few years.
    • Open Division: No age restrictions; open to all participants regardless of age, allowing surfers of all skill levels to compete against each other.
    • Masters Division: Surfers aged 35 and older, who may vary in skill level but have considerable experience in the sport.
    • Seniors Division: Surfers aged 50 and older, designed for older participants who may have a range of surfing abilities.
  • Skill
    • Novice / Beginner: Participants who have recently started surfing and are still learning the fundamentals of wave riding and basic maneuvers.
    • Intermediate: Surfers who have progressed beyond the beginner stage, capable of riding unbroken waves and attempting more advanced maneuvers.
    • Advanced: Skilled surfers who are proficient in various maneuvers, comfortable in challenging conditions, and display a higher level of technical ability.
    • Pro / Elite: Highly skilled and professional surfers, often competing at a national or international level with an exceptional level of expertise.
